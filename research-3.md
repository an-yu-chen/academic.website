# Science–Technology Linkages and Green Innovation Systems

## So, where do green technologies actually begin?

Let me start with green technologies. The real reason was my interest in how scientific knowledge translates into technological development.

My PhD thesis examined this directly:  
https://research.manchester.ac.uk/en/studentTheses/the-contribution-of-scientific-knowledge-to-green-technology-deve/ 

The one thing is clear: scientific knowledge plays a measurable role in enhancing technological impact in green patents.

That work helped pave the way for understanding how citation flows between papers and patents reflect deeper knowledge dynamics.

This is compounded by the fact that innovation increasingly depends on recombination across fields.

There is something I must emphasise: innovation is not only accumulation, but recombination of knowledge structures.

## So what does all of this imply in reflection?

It went far deeper than that.

In addition to this, I began to see how green technology development is shaped by both scientific diversity and institutional context.

That’s what I’m fighting for: a better understanding of how science contributes to solving environmental challenges.
