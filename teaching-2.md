# Classroom and Online Teaching Experience

## Teaching Across Different Environments

Teaching across both in-person and online learning environments has shaped how I understand communication, structure, and responsiveness in education. As we go along through different teaching contexts, it becomes clear that each setting requires a slightly different rhythm — yet both rely on clarity, preparation, and attentiveness.

From 2017 to 2020, I taught undergraduate Business Management Exercise classes at National Taiwan University, and also assisted in Organisational Behaviour teaching at the NTU School of Professional Education and Continuing Studies in Taipei.

Later, from September 2023 to January 2024, I worked as a Teaching Assistant in the Computer Science Department at The University of Manchester, supporting the undergraduate Database Systems course with Dr Gareth Henshall.

## Teaching as Preparation and Practice

Matter-of-factly, much of effective teaching happens before the classroom itself. In my experience, I often engaged in detailed preparation — coordinating with lecturers, reviewing reading materials, and building structured notes for coding examples and conceptual explanations.

I sometimes created my own notebooks to demonstrate how code functions in research and applied contexts. This process helped me put into practice a more grounded and example-driven approach to teaching.

It is essentially through preparation that teaching becomes more coherent. What is put into practice during a session is always shaped by what has been thought through beforehand.

## Working with Students and Teams

I enjoy working with others, and teaching has often been a collaborative effort. I have supported lab sessions, assisted in marking assignments on database design, and contributed to course delivery as part of wider teaching teams.

Teaching students from diverse academic and cultural backgrounds has also reminded me that learning is rarely linear. In spite of this complexity, I find it natural to adjust explanations, slow down where necessary, and help students connect abstract ideas with practical understanding.

## Reflection

Teaching, for me, is not only about delivering content but about building a structured yet flexible learning environment. It requires organisation, patience, and the ability to respond in real time.

I do not see teaching as separate from learning. As we go along, both continuously shape each other — in in-person and online spaces alike.
