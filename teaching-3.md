> *Teaching Series III — Interactive Learning, engagement, and dialogue in practice*

## Creating Engagement

One of the main things that struck me about teaching is the importance of interaction. In both classroom and online sessions, I try to foster an environment where students feel able to respond, question, and reflect.

I have committed to fostering a positive learning environment where feedback is not delayed, but immediate and meaningful.

## Teaching Methods in Practice

I frequently use Mentimeter, PointSolutions, and visual coding-based demonstrations to help students engage with content in real time. Canva is also a useful tool for organising responses and building shared mind maps during discussions.

I was particularly struck afresh during a workshop led by Mabel Sanchez, where I learned about flipping the classroom and designing more interactive sessions. The emphasis on student participation reshaped how I think about structure and delivery.

## Teaching as Dialogue

Teaching, for me, is not one-way transmission. It is a dialogue that evolves as we go along, shaped by student responses and shared understanding.
