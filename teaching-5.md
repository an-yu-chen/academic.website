# ✦ Feedback, Assessment, and Teaching Vision ✦

> *Teaching Series V — Assessment, reflection, and long-term direction in teaching practice*

---

## Feedback as Learning

To me, marking assignments and providing feedback is not separate from teaching — it is an extension of it. I enjoy knowledge transfer in seminars and tutorials, particularly where it connects directly with course goals and student development.

When I mark assignments, especially in areas such as database design, I aim to go beyond correction. I try to help students develop critical thinking, judgement, and discernment when engaging with complex or perplexing problems.

Feedback, in this sense, should be very much to the point: clear, constructive, and oriented towards improvement rather than judgement.

---

## Knowledge Transfer and Seminar Teaching

I have always enjoyed seminar-style teaching and discussion-based learning environments. These settings allow students to debate, question, and refine their understanding collectively.

Teaching in this format is less about delivering answers and more about guiding students through structured thinking. It allows ideas to spark interest and evolve through dialogue.

I also enjoy working with students from diverse backgrounds, where differences in perspective often lead to richer discussion and deeper engagement.

---

## Preparation, Organisation, and Teaching Practice

In my earlier teaching experience, I placed strong emphasis on preparation. I often coordinate with lecturers, review course content in detail, and prepare reading materials in advance.

At times, I also create my own structured notebooks for coding examples and research-based illustrations, particularly when demonstrating functions or data-driven concepts. This helps ensure that teaching delivery is both clear and practically grounded.

I am very organised, and I enjoy creating structured plans for teaching sessions and larger course components.

---

## Teaching in Context

During my time at the University of Manchester (Sept 2023–Jan 2024), I worked as a Teaching Assistant in the Computer Science Department, assisting in delivering the undergraduate Database Systems course and supporting lab sessions with Dr Gareth Henshall.

Earlier, I also taught undergraduate courses in Business Management Exercise (National Taiwan University, 2017–2020) and supported Organisational Behaviour teaching at NTU School of Professional Education and Continuing Studies.

These experiences helped me understand teaching as something both structured and relational — shaped by course design, but also by human interaction.

---

## Teaching Vision

I am interested in the management of IT and how this connects with teaching practice, particularly in structured problem-solving and analytical thinking.

Teaching, for me, is essentially about enabling students to build confidence in dealing with uncertainty and complexity. It is not only about transmitting knowledge, but about helping students develop the ability to think independently.

I have always worked well with others, and I enjoy helping students grow through guided learning, feedback, and discussion.

---

## Reflection

Teaching just comes naturally to me in the sense that it aligns with how I think and work: structured, reflective, and collaborative. I value both organisation and adaptability, and I continue to refine my approach through experience.

Ultimately, teaching is not only what I do — it is how I contribute to learning communities over time.
